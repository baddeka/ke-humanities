Enriching Wikidata:
Italian Renaissance Painters & Their Museums

A knowledge engineering project comparing two LLMs (Gemini and ChatGPT) as tools for identifying and filling factual gaps in Wikidata.

Wikidata SPARQL LLM Prompting RDF / Turtle Knowledge Graphs Italian Renaissance

Project Overview

Wikidata is one of the world's largest open knowledge bases, yet even well-studied domains like Italian Renaissance art contain systematic gaps. This project investigates three specific missing properties for a set of painters active between 1400 and 1560:

PropertyMeaningExample gap found
P1066 student of (teacher) Bernardino Luini (Q8537) — no teacher recorded
P195 collection (museum) Baronci Altarpiece (Q1778000) — no institution linked
P135 movement Boltraffio (Q442528), Luini (Q8537) — movement blank

We used SPARQL queries against the live Wikidata endpoint to discover these gaps empirically, then applied three prompting techniques (zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought) to two large language models — Google Gemini and OpenAI ChatGPT — to propose candidate values. Finally, we manually verified the suggestions and generated corrected RDF triples in Turtle format.

Research Hypothesis

Lesser-known painters in Leonardo da Vinci's Milanese circle are significantly underrepresented in Wikidata: many lack teacher-student links (P1066) and art movement assignments (P135). Chain-of-thought prompting elicits more accurate and better-justified values from LLMs than zero-shot or few-shot prompting for historically ambiguous cases.
