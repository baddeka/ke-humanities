Challenges & Limitations
Problems encountered across all project phases, from SPARQL querying to RDF generation to LLM evaluation.

1. Wikidata Data Model Surprises

Citizenship vs. birth country

Several painters active in Renaissance Italy are not recorded as Italian citizens in Wikidata. Bernardino Luini (Q8537) is a key example: he was born in Luino, a town that now falls in the Swiss canton of Ticino, and Wikidata records his citizenship accordingly. Our initial Q3 query filtered by wdt:P27 wd:Q38 (citizen of Italy) and returned zero results for him. We resolved this with a UNION clause covering birth in a place whose country is Italy, but the episode highlights that "Italian Renaissance painter" is an art-historical category that does not map cleanly onto Wikidata's political data model.

Q-ID collisions and wrong assumptions

Several Q-IDs assumed from prior knowledge turned out to be wrong. For instance, Q379948 was assumed to be Bernardino Luini based on common-knowledge lookups; in Wikidata it resolves to "San Pellegrino Pass" (a mountain pass). The correct Q-ID for Luini is Q8537. Similarly, Q530246 (assumed Boltraffio) resolved to "Yo-yo" and Q273840 (assumed Pinturicchio) resolved to an unrelated person. Lesson: never assume Q-IDs; always verify via SPARQL or the Wikidata search API before using them in queries or triples.

Multiple values for the same property

Wikidata allows — and in many cases requires — multiple values for the same property. Painters such as Domenico Ghirlandaio have two teachers listed under P1066. Paintings can have multiple P195 values when fragments are held by different institutions. Our SPARQL results included duplicate rows (one per teacher, one per movement) that required deduplication in post-processing.

2. LLM Hallucinations

Invented Q-IDs

Both models produced plausible-sounding but non-existent Wikidata identifiers. The most dangerous class of error: the Q-ID looks syntactically valid (e.g., wd:Q998877) and could be silently accepted by a human reviewer in a hurry. Every Q-ID must be checked against the live endpoint.

Wrong property IDs

P135 (movement) and P136 (genre) are adjacent property numbers. Both models confused them in at least one response. P21 (sex/gender) was used instead of P27 (citizenship) in another case. One-digit errors in property IDs produce statements that are syntactically well-formed but semantically wrong — and therefore hard to catch without knowing the property vocabulary.

Overconfidence on uncertain facts

The zero-shot prompt about Luini's teacher typically received a confident "Leonardo da Vinci" with a high confidence rating. In reality, the art-historical record is less clear: Luini was in Leonardo's circle and shows strong Leonardesque influence, but direct documentation of an apprenticeship relationship is lacking. Chain-of-thought prompting was better at surfacing this uncertainty.

3. Historical Ambiguity

Teacher-student relationships in Renaissance workshops

Renaissance workshops did not operate like modern schools. A painter might work alongside a master (bottega), copy their style, and be strongly influenced without being a formal apprentice. The Wikidata property P1066 ("student of") conflates formal apprenticeship with informal influence. For painters like Luini, who absorbed Leonardo's style but may not have trained formally under him, any P1066 value requires a qualifier explaining the nature of the relationship.

Dispersed and fragmented artworks

The Baronci Altarpiece (Q1778000) was partly destroyed in an earthquake and its surviving fragments are distributed across multiple institutions. Wikidata can express this with multiple P195 values, each optionally qualified with P518 (applies to part). However, the exact provenance of each fragment remains contested in the scholarly literature, making it impossible to add P195 values with high confidence without museum-level archival research.

4. SPARQL Query Engineering

Timeout and endpoint limits

The Wikidata SPARQL endpoint enforces a 60-second query timeout. Complex queries combining multiple OPTIONAL clauses, SERVICE wikibase:label blocks, and UNION patterns risk timeout. We mitigated this with LIMIT clauses and by splitting complex queries into focused sub-queries.

SSL certificate revocation on Windows

Executing SPARQL queries via curl on Windows failed initially with error CRYPT_E_REVOCATION_OFFLINE — the system could not check certificate revocation status offline. The workaround was adding --ssl-no-revoke to all curl calls. This is acceptable in a controlled academic environment but would need proper certificate management in production.

5. Scope Limitations

This project covers three properties (P1066, P195, P135) and twelve painters. Wikidata contains thousands of Italian Renaissance painters and tens of thousands of artworks. The gap analysis presented here is a sample, not a complete audit. Systematic enrichment would require automated pipelines combining SPARQL-based gap detection with LLM suggestion and human expert review — a larger-scale knowledge engineering effort beyond the scope of a student project.

For the oral defence: The two most discussion-worthy challenges are (1) the Q-ID verification problem — LLMs produce confident-looking identifiers that require systematic SPARQL verification — and (2) the historical ambiguity of teacher-student relationships in Renaissance workshops, which shows that P1066 is not a simple factual gap but a modelling decision.
